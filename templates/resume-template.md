## Tailoring Plan

- **No "Target Role" section.** Do not add a section that names the company/vacancy at the top of the resume — it reads as filler.
- **Career Expectations must stay meta, not vacancy-bound.** Write it about the value the candidate brings from experience + motivation (what kind of engineer he is, what problems he's drawn to, what he leaves behind). Do NOT name the company, the role title, the domain (travel/subscription/etc.), or "Barcelona hybrid" inside it.
- Rewrite the summary for this role (the summary may use domain language; Career Expectations may not).
- Reorder experience bullets by role relevance.
- Pull only the strongest 2-4 supporting examples.
- Adjust terminology to the vacancy wording.
- Drop weaker or distracting details.

## Final Checks

- Remove placeholders like `[Company Name]` / `[Title]`.
- Confirm there is **no "Target Role" section**.
- Confirm **Career Expectations is meta**: no company/role/domain/location named inside it.
- **No em/long dashes (—) in prose** (Summary, Career Expectations, descriptions). Use a comma, colon, or full stop. (En-dashes in dates/titles like "2024 – 2026" are the existing house style and stay.)
- **No LLM-style filler.** Avoid phrasings like "what drives me is", "force multiplier", "at home in", "holds up under real-world scale", "leaving things better than I found them", and stacked rule-of-three clichés. Write plainly, the way the candidate would actually speak.
- Keep the document readable in one screen per role section.
- Make sure the profile does not look split between too many directions.
