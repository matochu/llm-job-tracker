# Outreach Style

Outreach workflows prepare manual message drafts only. They do not send messages, connection requests, emails, LinkedIn messages, or applications. The user is the only person who writes/sends messages.

The expected deliverable is an updated `### Manual Message Drafts` section in the relevant company prep notes. User-facing replies should summarize what draft text was prepared and where it was saved, not present sending as completed or ready.

## Default Language

- LinkedIn and recruiter messages: English.
- User-facing summaries: follow `config/language.md`.

## General Rules

- Keep messages short: 2-3 short paragraphs.
- Ask for context first when the contact is weak.
- Ask for referral only when the contact is warm or clearly relevant.
- Mention one specific reason the company/role is relevant.
- Mention 2-4 strongest matching skills, not the whole CV.
- Avoid desperate tone, generic praise, and long autobiography.

## Message Types

### Recruiter

Draft a short manual message asking whether the user should share a CV or apply through ATS first. Mention role title, location fit, and top stack match.

### Engineering Manager / Tech Lead

Ask for a quick profile look or direction to the right hiring contact. Emphasize technical match and ownership.

### Employee / Referral Contact

Ask for a reality check first. If they respond positively, then ask whether referral makes sense.

### Founder / Leadership

Use only for early startups, devtools/platform companies, or direct founder-posted jobs. Be concise and product-specific.

## Follow-Up

If no reply after a few days, prepare one short follow-up draft. Do not send repeated follow-ups unless the user asks.
